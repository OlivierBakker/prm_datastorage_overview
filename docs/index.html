A hub for all things datamanagement

Bad data storage is a cause of much frustration among research staff. It can be very hard to find the data you want and it involves inquiring with many people, sometimes over several days, before you find the data you need. This leads to unnecessary delays, bottlenecks and potential mistakes due to unclear annotation and organization. In the long term, poorly organized data can also give issues with reproducibility of our research. This is especially the case when the person who performed the research has already left, and no one really knows anymore where to find what they need. This goes against the FAIR principles we are aiming to adhere to. In addition, poor data storage leads to increased costs if many unused, deprecated or duplicated datasets are still kept. These issues are largely due to a lack of a proper standard for the organization of data on our research clusters and the large amount of strain this puts on individual data managers. This website provides a hub to all things datamangement.
